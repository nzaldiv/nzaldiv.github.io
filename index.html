These are most recent jobs and activities were I developed my skills as an engineer.

Top-Layer Embroidery of Pneumatic-Based Soft Actuators

As a student researcher, my focus was on designing and manufacturing soft robotic actuators to achieve various movement modalities. The goal was to establish a heuristic design approach. By applying theoretical models that govern the motion behavior of pneumatic-based soft actuators, my research aimed to program the volume-changing response that occurs when compressed air is injected into the soft actuator through selective programming of the fabric surface stiffnesses using embroidery techniques.

Engineering Assistant

Assisted with encroachment permit processing for the Engineering Division of the City of Richmond. Under the supervision of a Civil PE Engineer, I utilized AutoCAD to conceptualize Traffic Control Plans (TCP). I also maintained Public Safety by updating existing traffic layouts to meet CA-MUTCD standards and oversaw their installation in the Public Right-of-Way.

Water Distribution System

Designed a water distribution system for a sustainable home, where I considered factors such as water pressure, flow rate, and availability of hot water. The primary objective was to ensure water delivery within a 10-second timeframe while optimizing efficiency and cost-effectiveness.
